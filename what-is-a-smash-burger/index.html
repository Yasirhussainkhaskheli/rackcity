What Is A Smash Burger?

A smash burger is a beef patty pressed thin on a hot grill. The pressure creates a crispy, caramelised crust. The centre stays juicy. Smash burgers are different from regular thick burgers.

Comparison Table

Pressed thin on the grill

Thick patty, not pressed

Crispy edges

No crispy edge

Cooked fast at high heat

Cooked slower at medium heat

Usually double or triple patty

Often single patty

What Makes a Smash Burger Good?

The smash technique creates the Maillard reaction – browning that adds flavour. A good smash burger has:

A thin, lacy edge.

A juicy centre.

Melted cheese covering the patty.

Toasted brioche bun.

Simple toppings.
